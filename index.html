Overview

My research centers on global change biology, with the goal of providing both theoretical insights and practical solutions to the challenges posed by a rapidly changing climate. Over the years, I have advanced our understanding of how climate change impacts phenology and biodiversity, with significant implications for both natural and social systems. My current work further integrates AI methodologies with ecological research. My future research will explore the novel fields of phenology of biodiversity, multimodal modeling, and ecological forecasting, all seeking to bridge data science and global change biology. I am deeply committed to involving underrepresented students in STEM, ensuring they are actively engaged in my research projects.
